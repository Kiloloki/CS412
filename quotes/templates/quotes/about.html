About Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein (1879-1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist best known for the theory of relativity and the famous equation E = mcÂ². He also made key contributions to quantum mechanics, especially the photoelectric effect, which earned him the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics. Born in the German Empire, Einstein moved to Switzerland in 1895, later working at the Swiss Patent Office. His 1905 "miracle year" produced four groundbreaking papers, including those on special relativity and Brownian motion. In 1915, he introduced general relativity, transforming our understanding of gravity and the cosmos. He emigrated to the U.S. in 1933 due to Nazi persecution and became an American citizen in 1940. In later years, Einstein opposed the probabilistic nature of quantum mechanics and pursued a unified field theory, though unsuccessfully. Despite growing isolated, his legacy profoundly shaped modern physics.
